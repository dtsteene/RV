
# Modeling the Heart's Hidden Hero: The Right Ventricle


+++{"part" : "abstract"}
This thesis investigates the mechanical function of the right ventricle (RV), often overlooked in favor of the left ventricle despite its critical role in cardiovascular health. 

The project employs the finite element method (FEM) to create sophisticated bi-ventricular models, utilizing the **FEniCSx** library under the supervision of **Simula Research Laboratory**
+++

:::{tip} The Core Question

**Do standard right-ventricular (RV) pressure–volume (PV) loops accurately reflect the mechanical work generated by the RV myocardium?**

Specifically, we investigate how the shared septum, loaded by high left-ventricular pressure, affects the "energy accounting" of the RV. This thesis documents the mathematical theory, the computational implementation, and the experimental results used to answer this question.
:::
