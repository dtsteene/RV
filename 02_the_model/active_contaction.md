# Active Contraction

While the passive model describes how the heart resists stretch, the active model describes how it generates force. This is the process of **Excitation-Contraction Coupling (ECC)**.



## Active Stress Approach

We model contraction using the **active stress formulation**. We decompose the total stress in the tissue ($S_{total}$) into a passive elastic component and an active contractile component:

$$
S_{total} = S_{passive} + S_{active}
$$

The active stress is generated specifically along the muscle fibers. We define it as:

$$
S_{active} = T_a (f_0 \otimes f_0)
$$

Where:

**$T_a$ (Active Tension)**
: The scalar magnitude of force generated by the sarcomeres (in kPa).

**$f_0$ (Fiber Vector)**
: The local unit vector defining the orientation of the muscle fibers.

**$f_0 \otimes f_0$ (Tensor Product)**
: A structural tensor that directs this tension purely along the fiber axis, ensuring the heart contracts anisotropically.

## From Electricity to Force ($T_a$)

The magnitude of the active tension, $T_a$, is time-dependent and driven by the electrical activation of the cell.

In our model, we determine $T_a(t)$ using a simplified activation function that mimics the **calcium transient** in a cardiomyocyte. This function, `get_activation(t)`, provides the input signal that drives the mechanical contraction in our simulation loop.
